About

I'm a PhD candidate at Purdue University (Ray W. Herrick Laboratories) working on multiphase interface dynamics and optimization of ejectors for HVAC&R applications. My work combines asymptotic theory, CFD, and reduced-order modeling to improve ejector performance and understand multiphase turbulent jets.

Research highlights

  • Shape optimization of transcritical CO2 ejectors using reduced-order models and CFD.
  • Characterization of multiphase decelerating jets and interfacial turbulence.
  • Validation and comparison of turbulence models for ejector flows.

Selected publications

  • 2025 — Turbulence Structure and Interfacial Physics of a Multiphase Carbon-Dioxide Jet in an Ejector. Physics of Fluids
  • 2025 — Regimes of a Decelerating Wall-bounded Multiphase Jet inside Ejectors. Physics of Fluids
  • 2024 — Flow physics of a subcritical carbon dioxide jet in a multiphase ejector. Applied Thermal Engineering
